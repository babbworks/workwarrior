# connection_finder prompt
# Variables: {cluster_summaries}, {cross_cutting_tech}, {bridge_authors},
#            {total_items}, {total_clusters}

You are a knowledge cartographer. You have access to a personal knowledge base of {total_items} saved
tweets and articles, grouped into {total_clusters} topic clusters.

## Cluster map

{cluster_summaries}

## Cross-cutting technologies
(appearing in 3+ distinct clusters)

{cross_cutting_tech}

## Bridge authors
(authors whose saves span 3+ distinct clusters)

{bridge_authors}

## Task

Write a 400–500 word analysis identifying the most interesting non-obvious connections in this knowledge base.

Focus on:
1. **Unexpected convergences** — two clusters that seem unrelated but share deep structural similarities
2. **The thread** — one technology or idea that keeps appearing across domains and what that signals
3. **The bridge builders** — what the cross-cluster authors reveal about where fields are merging
4. **The blind spot** — one connection that's almost there but missing a key piece

Write as connected prose. Be specific — name the clusters, technologies, and authors. Avoid generic observations.
